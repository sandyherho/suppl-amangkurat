COMPARATIVE SUMMARY:

Shannon Entropy (S):
  Quantifies spatial information spread.
  Higher S → more delocalized field.

Rényi Entropies (H_α):
  H₀.₅: emphasizes rare events
  H₂: focuses on high-probability regions (collision entropy)
  H₁₀: approximates min-entropy (maximum probability)

Tsallis Entropies (S_q):
  Capture non-extensive statistics.
  Useful for systems with long-range correlations.

Composite Entropy (C):
  Weighted combination: C = 0.5*S + 0.3*H₂ + 0.2*S₂
  Balances spatial spread, concentration, non-extensivity.
  Single metric for overall complexity comparison.

Physical Interpretation:
  Lower entropy → coherent, localized structures (kink)
  Higher entropy → spread-out, dispersed fields (linear wave)
  Oscillating entropy → periodic dynamics (breather)
  Complex entropy evolution → nonlinear interactions (collision)
